## Distribution of vehicle types by State

This stacked bar chart shows the distribution of vehicle types by state, expressed in percentages. For each U.S. state, the total bar equals 100% of vehicles, divided into four categories: PHEV (plug-in hybrid electric vehicles), HEV (hybrid electric vehicles), EV (fully electric vehicles), and gasoline vehicles.

<img width="1032" height="413" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/94a35f3a-fd6e-44f2-a47e-f7a8ad1e46e3" />

- Gasoline vehicles still dominate in every state, while electrified vehicles (EVs, HEVs, and PHEVs) make up a smaller but varying share depending on the state. States such as the District of Columbia and California show higher proportions of electric and hybrid vehicles, reflecting stronger adoption of cleaner technologies. In contrast, many other states have larger gasoline shares and lower electrification, illustrating regional differences in infrastructure, policy, and consumer preferences.

## Distribution of alternative fuel use in the U.S.

This reflects the already established infrastructure for ethanol (such as the use of E85 in flex-fuel cars), while hydrogen still faces production and distribution challenges.

### Infrastructure Planning

- States with high EV share (CA, DC, WA, HI, CO, NJ) need continued expansion of fast chargers and grid upgrades to keep pace with adoption.
- Large, low-penetration states (TX, FL) are high-potential markets, these require proactive charging deployment now to avoid future bottlenecks. Moreover, they will see rapid percentage growth as adoption accelerates.
- Investment should balance where adoption is already high to avoid overload and where absolute EV counts are large but relative penetration is low to unlock growth.
